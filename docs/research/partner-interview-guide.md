# ICE Assist Partner Interview Guide

## Purpose

Use this guide to validate the MVP with legal aid providers, accredited representatives, shelter staff, and navigators before implementation decisions harden.

## Interview goals

- identify the documents that matter most
- understand the most dangerous misunderstandings
- define the safest and most useful referral and escalation handoff
- validate the referral and escalation model

## Participants

- legal aid attorneys
- DOJ-accredited representatives
- shelter or respite-center staff
- case navigators and legal screeners

## Core questions for all partners

1. What paperwork do people most often bring to you confused about?
2. Which misunderstandings lead to the most harm or the most avoidable chaos?
3. What information do you wish a client already understood before they reached you?
4. What information do you need captured before intake and what information should not be captured?
5. Which situations should always trigger direct human escalation?

## Legal aid and accredited representative questions

1. Which documents are safe to explain in a structured way and which are too risky?
2. What confirmed details does a person need in hand before they contact your team?
3. What wording would make you trust the app more and what wording would immediately worry you?
4. What is the minimum acceptable disclaimer boundary for a tool like this?
5. Which referral sources do you consider reliable enough for user-facing display?

## Shelter and navigator questions

1. At what moment do people ask for help with documents?
2. Are they usually alone, with family, or with a helper when this happens?
3. Do they usually have their own device, share one, or depend on staff devices?
4. What part of the workflow takes the most repeated staff time?
5. What would make the app helpful in your setting rather than one more burden?

## Operational questions

1. How often do contact details, service availability, or local partner coverage change?
2. Who could help validate referral data on an ongoing basis?
3. How should the app show freshness so users and staff can judge whether a listing is current?
4. What is the safest way for a user to arrive with the original document and a clear next-step understanding?

## Prototype questions

If showing a mock flow or prototype, ask:

1. Where would a user misunderstand this screen?
2. Where would a user over-trust the app?
3. Which part of this flow would fail in a real shelter or intake setting?
4. What should the app refuse to do here?
5. What one thing would make this immediately more useful?

## Red flags to listen for

- partners say the app sounds like legal advice
- partners distrust the referral sourcing model
- partners need data the app cannot safely collect
- partners believe misclassification risk is too high for certain documents
- partners think the product would create new intake confusion rather than reduce it

## Output format after each interview

- top 5 documents mentioned
- top 5 harmful misunderstandings mentioned
- must-have confirmed details before referral or intake
- mandatory escalation triggers mentioned
- concerns about trust, privacy, or misuse

## Decision rule

If multiple partners independently identify the same document class, harm pattern, or escalation need, treat it as a design input rather than anecdotal feedback.
